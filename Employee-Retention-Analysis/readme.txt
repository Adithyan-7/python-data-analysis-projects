## 📊 Project Overview

This project aims to analyze and predict employee retention using Logistic Regression. 
The objective is to determine whether an employee will leave the company (attrition) 
or continue working based on various workplace factors.

Employee retention is a critical challenge for organizations. By identifying 
key factors influencing employee turnover, companies can take proactive steps 
to improve employee satisfaction and reduce attrition.

---

## 🔎 Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

Exploratory Data Analysis was performed to identify variables that have a direct 
impact on employee retention. Bar charts were plotted to analyze:

- The impact of salary levels on employee retention
- The relationship between department and employee turnover

The analysis revealed that:
- Employees with low salaries are more likely to leave.
- Certain departments show higher attrition rates.

---

## 🤖 Model Building

A Logistic Regression model was built using important features such as:

- Satisfaction level
- Average monthly hours
- Promotion in the last 5 years
- Salary level
- Department

Categorical variables were converted into dummy variables using one-hot encoding.

---

## 📈 Model Performance

The model was trained using an 80-20 train-test split and achieved an accuracy of approximately **79%**, indicating a strong ability to predict employee retention.

---

## 🎯 Conclusion

The project demonstrates how machine learning can be used to predict employee turnover and help organizations make data-driven HR decisions.
